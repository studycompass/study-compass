How Your GPA Actually Gets Calculated

📅 June 28, 2026 · ☕ 5 min read · 🧭 Study Compass Team
🧮

Few things cause more academic anxiety than grade calculations. Whether you are staring at a 42-out-of-50 quiz wondering if it is an A or a B, or trying to figure out what GPA you need next semester to keep a scholarship, the math behind grades often feels more opaque than it should.

A percentage is simply a way of expressing a score as a portion of 100. The formula is straightforward: divide the score you earned by the total possible score, then multiply by 100. If you scored 42 out of 50, the calculation is (42 ÷ 50) × 100 = 84%.

Your Grade Point Average (GPA) compresses all your letter grades into a single number on a 4.0 scale. Each letter grade corresponds to a fixed number of grade points: an A is 4.0, a B is 3.0, a C is 2.0, a D is 1.0, and an F is 0. To compute your GPA, multiply each course's grade points by its credit hours, add those products together, then divide by the total credit hours.
